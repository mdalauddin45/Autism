No Sign of Autism

If your quiz results show no signs of autism, it means that your baby is not currently exhibiting the common indicators associated with ASD. This is a positive sign, and we encourage you to continue monitoring your baby’s development through regular interactions and play.

Remember, every child develops at their own pace, and the absence of early signs of autism is not a guarantee of future development. Regular check-ups with your pediatrician are essential to track your baby’s growth and development.


What’s Next?

Stay Informed: Learn about developmental milestones to watch for as your baby grows.
Regular Check-Ups: Keep up with your baby’s scheduled well-child visits.
Engage & Interact: Spend quality time playing and interacting with your baby to encourage their social and communication skills.


Need More Information?

If you have any questions or concerns about your baby’s development, don’t hesitate to reach out to your healthcare provider. Early intervention can make a significant difference, so it’s important to seek professional advice if you have any doubts.
