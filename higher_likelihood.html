High (Higher Likelihood of Autism)

Treatment:

  • Comprehensive Therapy: Engage in comprehensive therapies including ABA, speech therapy, and occupational therapy tailored to individual needs; consulting an autism specialist or doctor is highly recommended for developing an effective treatment plan.
  • Behavioral Intervention: Use behavioral interventions to address specific challenges and reinforce positive behaviors.
  • Specialized Medical Care: Consult with healthcare providers who specialize in autism to address any co-occurring conditions.

Education:

  • Specialized Autism School: Consider specialized autism schools that provide structured environments and tailored instruction.
  • Inclusive Education with Support: If in a mainstream school, ensure significant support through aides, specialized programs, and tailored instruction.
  • Transition Programs: Utilize transition programs that prepare for life beyond school, including vocational training and independent living skills.

Community and Social Support:

  • Autism-Specific Support Groups: Engage with autism-specific support groups for both the individual and family to navigate challenges and share resources.
  • Community Integration Programs: Participate in community integration programs that facilitate involvement in social, recreational, and vocational activities.
  • Respite Care: Access respite care services to provide breaks for caregivers and ensure consistent support for the individual.
